THE DEMENTIA HELPER

Your guide to easier and better browsing

 

Dementia is a umbrella term for a set of symptoms that is linked with brain disorders and memory loss. When people talk about dementia, they often mean dementia caused by Alzheimer’s disease. People suffering from dementia caused by Alzheimer’s has a reduced short-term memory. This includes forgetting appointments, keeping track of important items such as wallets and mobile devices. Dementia is unfortunately an incurable disease, and the patients conditions gradually worsen over time. In the videos below we meet Wendy, Keith, and Christopher. They tell us what it is like living with dementia, and how it effects their everyday life.

According the world health organisation, there are estimated over 4.7 million people worldwide who suffer from dementia, and the number is increasing as people live longer. The effect on dementia on peoples life is not limited to the patients only. Their family members often feel frustrated, angry and helpless. There are several national and international organisations whom have dedicated their life to help people with dementia and their families. If you want to read more about dementia and the organisation that work with it, here are some useful links: Dementia.com, alz.org .
